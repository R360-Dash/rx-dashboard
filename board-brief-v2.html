Executive Summary
  • ↑
    Show Cycle 2026 closed strong: Net income through April (all show activity complete) is $270,657. Revenue grew 30% to $1.34M YTD with gross margin of 44.4% (+12.7pp vs FY2025). The show itself generated $552K gross profit (42% margin) in SC2026 vs $211K (22.9%) in SC2025 — a $341K improvement. Expected full-year FY2026 net (including 8 months of remaining overhead + City Series): $(227K).
  • !
    Cash runway is tight: Current bank balance is $146,720. Based on the forward forecast, cash is projected to turn negative in October 2026 without additional capital or 2027 show deposits. Expected full-year SG&A burden ($804K) against $167K of remaining City Series revenue creates an ~$(450K) EBITDA drag through year-end.
  • ▼
    Full-year overhead exceeds show revenue: Even with a profitable show event, 12 months of SG&A ($804K expected) vs. a March-concentrated revenue cycle keeps the full fiscal year net negative. Reducing this gap requires either growing City Series (planned $167K remaining) or securing 2027 advance deposits to bridge H2 2026.

Key Risks & Recommended Actions
🚨
Cash Shortfall — October 2026
Cash turns negative ~October 15–20. The projected gap through year-end is ~$112K. Actions: (1) Accelerate A/R collection ($159K outstanding), (2) Secure 2027 show advance deposits from signed prospects, (3) Evaluate SG&A reduction Aug–Dec.
⚠️
Full-Year Expected Net: $(227K) — Overhead Gap
Even with a $271K profitable show (Jan–Apr), the full FY2026 is expected to end at $(227K) net as 8 months of SGA ($500K budget) and CEA ($48K) outweigh the $167K remaining CS revenue. Breaking this pattern requires either growing City Series revenue meaningfully (currently budgeted at $167K remaining) or right-sizing off-peak overhead.
📊
2027 Pipeline Strong but Revenue Recognition is Late-Cycle
HubSpot shows $502K in signed/won deals for 2027, with Show sponsors at ~$440K. Revenue concentration patterns suggest most won't be recognized until March 2027. Deposits received now help cash but don't flow through P&L until service delivery.
✅
Show Economics Are Working — Model Validated
The show itself delivered a $271K net profit through April — up from a $(517K) full-year loss in FY2025. Gross margin improved from 31.7% to 44.4% (+12.7pp). Show GP grew from $256K (FY2025) to $517K expected (FY2026E) — a +101% improvement. The gap to full profitability is an overhead coverage problem, not a show-economics problem.
